Darién-Gap (Kolumbien/Panama)

The Darién Gap, a densely forested and nearly impassable region between Panama and Colombia, is a perilous crossing attempted by many migrants fleeing various Latin American countries in hopes of reaching the United States. The extreme weather conditions, thick vegetation, steep hills, dangerous animals, the risk of disease, and the threat posed by criminal groups result in many migrants dying or suffering serious injuries during the journey.

Libya-Italy

3 to 7 days

from Libya to Europe with boats

Many migrants attempting to reach Europe find themselves in desperate situations off the coast of Libya, often relying on rescues from European or private aid organizations. However, these rescue efforts are frequently obstructed by the Libyan coastguard, which intercepts boats and returns many migrants to so-called detention centers in Libya, where they live in inhumane conditions and are often subjected to abuse​.

Reports indicate that these detention facilities are overcrowded and lack basic necessities such as food and medical care. Migrants face severe human rights violations, including physical violence, sexual abuse, and insufficient access to essential resources.
